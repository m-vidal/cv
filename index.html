About me

MSc Math Stat | B. Comm | B. Music

My research revolves around high-dimensional statistical models, specifically principal and independent component analysis for object-oriented data. I specialize in nonlinear optimization, using denoising and smoothing techniques through spline and wavelet approximations. My primary emphasis is harnessing statistical methods to analyze complex neuroimaging data, particularly EEG and pupillometry, aiming to reveal the neuromodulatory effects of motor intention in natural behaviors.
